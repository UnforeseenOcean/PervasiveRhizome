Rosa Menkman is a Dutch art theorist, curator and visual artist specialising in glitch art and resolution theory. Menkman has curated several international exhibitions of other artists' work.[1]

Glitch art[edit]

Menkman's research into the emerging form of glitch art was published as the book The Glitch Moment(um) at the University of Amsterdam, by the Institute of Network Cultures.

She also wrote the Glitch Studies Manifesto[2] in the same year. This manifesto was awarded 'best practice' by Virtueel Platform, then sector institute for e-culture in the Netherlands.[3]

The publication of The Glitch Moment(um) coincided with the GLI.TC/H festival, organized by Menkman in collaboration with American artists Nick Briz and Jon Satrom. The first GLI.TC/H festival in 2010 (Chicago) was followed by a second and third edition in 2011 (Chicago, Amsterdam, Birmingham) and 2012 (Chicago).[4]
